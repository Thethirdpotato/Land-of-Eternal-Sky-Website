History

“Би бол Бурханы шийтгэл...Хэрэв та нар их нүгэл үйлдээгүй
бол Бурхан илгээхгүй байсан. Чамайг над шиг шийтгэнэ." - ᠴᠢᠩᠭᠢᠰ ᠬᠠᠭᠠᠨ

“I am the punishment of God...If you had not committed great sins,
God would not have sent a punishment like me upon you.” - Genghis Khan

1206

Genghis Khan establishes the Mongol Empire, uniting various nomadic tribes under his leadership.

1227

Genghis Khan passes away, and his descendants, particularly his grandsons Kublai Khan and Hulagu Khan, continue expanding the empire.

1260s

The Mongol Empire splits into four khanates: the Yuan Dynasty in China, the Ilkhanate in the Middle East, the Chagatai Khanate in Central Asia, and the Golden Horde in Eastern Europe.

1368

The Yuan Dynasty collapses, marking the end of Mongol rule in China.

17th Century

Mongolia falls under the influence of the Qing Dynasty in China.

Early 1900s

Mongolia declares independence from the Qing Dynasty, establishing the Bogd Khanate. The People's Revolution leads to the establishment of the Mongolian People's Republic, aligning with Soviet influence.

1990

Mongolia transitions to a multi-party democracy, leading to the end of one-party rule.

Present

Mongolia continues its path of democratic governance while facing challenges related to economic development, environmental conservation, and maintaining its unique cultural heritage.
